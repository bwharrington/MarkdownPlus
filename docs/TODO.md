### Fundamental Properties and Types

A black hole is defined by its  **event horizon** —the point of no return. For a non-rotating, uncharged (Schwarzschild) black hole, the radius is given by:

[ r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \approx 2.95 \frac{M}{M_\odot} ] km,

where ( G ) is the gravitational constant, ( M ) is mass, ( c ) is the speed of light, and ( M_\odot ) is one solar mass. The central **singularity** has infinite density and curvature according to general relativity.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_hole)
